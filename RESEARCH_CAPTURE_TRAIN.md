- On a toy distribution, minimizing KL (mass-covering) gives acceptance alpha=50.2% and reverse KL (mode-seeking) gives 50.8%, whereas minimizing Total Variation distance maximizes distributional overlap and reaches alpha=60.2%, illustrating why acceptance-targeted losses beat KL for capacity-limited drafts.
- EAGLE-3 abandons feature (hidden-state) regression loss and trains the draft head purely for direct next-token prediction, which is what unlocks data-scaling gains; the removed feature-prediction loss l_fea is the key change from EAGLE-1/2.
- Instead of only the top-layer feature, EAGLE-3 fuses low-, mid-, and high-level hidden states from the target's forward pass: three k-dim vectors l, m, h are concatenated into a 3k vector and passed through an FC layer to reduce back to k. This dictates exactly what must be cached offline (three layer feature maps, not just the last hidden state).
- EAGLE-3's disclosed training recipe: AdamW optimizer, betas (0.9, 0.95), gradient clipping 0.5, learning rate 5e-5, using ShareGPT and UltraChat-200K as training data. This is a concrete, implementable hyperparameter set for a draft-head trainer.
- EAGLE-3 was trained with roughly 8x more data than EAGLE and yields ~1.4x latency speedup over EAGLE-2 at batch size 1, and a data-scaling law where more draft-training data proportionally increases speedup (an effect absent in original EAGLE) - supporting the case for larger draft-training corpora.
- On a 70B target (LLaMA-Instruct 3.3 70B), EAGLE-3 reaches ~5.0x speedup (Fig 2 shows EAGLE-3 5.0x vs EAGLE-2 4.85x on L33 70B), demonstrating draft-head acceleration scales to large targets at temperature 0.
- FastMTP trains the MTP draft head purely with a token-level weighted cross-entropy loss (exponential decay across the K prediction steps, beta=0.6), NOT hidden-state/feature regression or logit KD; only the MTP head is updated (210.8M params, <3% of the 7833.4M model) while the backbone stays frozen.
- Self-distilled training data (the target model generates all responses itself) outperforms using original fixed dataset responses: self-data fine-tuning gives 1.81x vs fixed-data fine-tuning 1.67x speedup, indicating on-policy self-generation from the target materially improves draft alignment even for a cross-entropy objective. The corpus is 389.4K English+Chinese samples spanning general knowledge, math, and coding.
- Convergence is cheap and fast: 3 epochs over 389.4K self-distilled samples, cosine schedule with peak LR 5e-5 and 0.05 warmup ratio, AdamW (0.9, 0.95), global batch size 64, prediction depth K=3, completing in under one day on a single H20 GPU; this is a warm-start fine-tune of an existing DeepSeek-V3-style MTP head, not from-scratch.
- Fine-tuning recovers draft acceptance that collapses when a DeepSeek MTP head is naively reused recursively: acceptance rate rises from 70%->81% at the 1st draft token, 11%->56% at the 2nd, and 2%->36% at the 3rd, lifting mean accepted length tau up to 3.16 (math) and yielding an average 2.03x decode speedup (vs vanilla MTP reuse's 1.21x) at K=3 with lossless output.
- Draft-time cost is reduced by restricting the MTP head's output logits to a language-aware high-frequency vocabulary subset (optimal 32k tokens for English, 16k for Chinese, out of 152k) during drafting only; verification retains the full vocabulary, preserving losslessness, adding ~16% throughput at K=3 with negligible acceptance loss (e.g. 32k costs only 0.068 tokens of acceptance length).
- Quantizing the TARGET model has minimal impact on a draft head's average accepted length τ: an EAGLE-2 draft trained against the FP16 target retains nearly the same accept behavior on W4A16 and W8A8 quantized targets without any retraining, indicating the quantization-induced output/hidden-state distribution shift is small enough that draft accept rate is largely preserved.
- The recommended configuration keeps the draft model at FP16 (unquantized, unmodified) rather than adapting it to the quantized target, because quantizing the draft itself substantially degrades acceptance — implying the cheapest recovery path for a quantized target is to leave the high-precision draft as-is, not retrain/quantize it.
- For 4-bit weight targets the limiting factor is NOT lost acceptance length but the verification-to-decoding time ratio Tv(n)/Tt, which rises to ~1.8 for W4A16 (vs <1.2 for FP16/W8A8 at tree size 60), meaning quantized targets favor smaller draft trees / fewer forward passes rather than larger ones.
- Their hierarchical framework (HierSpec) reaches 98.19 tokens/s = 2.78x over W4A16 vanilla autoregressive on Llama-3-70B (A100), and 1.31x over the best EAGLE-2 configuration, giving a concrete achievable speedup figure for a ~70B quantized target with a spec-decode draft.
- Warm-start the existing DeepSeek MTP/DSpark head and fine-tune it for only 1-3 epochs offline — do NOT train from scratch. This is the leanest, highest-ROI method and is demonstrated specifically on a DeepSeek-V3 MTP head. — [https://arxiv.org/pdf/2602.23881](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2602.23881) _(conf high)_
- For a DeepSeek MTP-lineage head, the training objective should be per-depth TOKEN cross-entropy (decay-weighted across K steps), NOT hidden-state feature regression. SOTA (EAGLE-3) has moved away from feature-prediction toward direct token prediction. — [https://arxiv.org/pdf/2412.19437](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2412.19437) _(conf high)_
- Augmenting token CE with a logit-KD / KL distillation term (and optionally a composite MSE+CE) further raises acceptance and is the mechanism that transfers the MODIFIED target's output distribution into the head. — [https://arxiv.org/pdf/2602.23881](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2602.23881) _(conf high)_
- The single most important data decision is SELF-DISTILLATION / on-policy regeneration: the MODIFIED (pruned+quantized) target must generate the training responses itself. This is what recovers the distribution shift; a static offline corpus alone underperforms. — [https://arxiv.org/pdf/2509.18362](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2509.18362) _(conf high)_
- Concrete converged hyperparameters from disclosed recipes: 1-3 epochs for a warm-started MTP head (10 for scratch), LR 1e-4 to 5e-5 with cosine/linear decay, AdamW(0.9,0.95), small global batch (64), prediction depth K=3. — [https://arxiv.org/html/2603.18567v1](https://arxiv.org/html/2603.18567v1) _(conf high)_
- Dataset size: 68K-390K samples (or ~1.4M conversations at the high end) is sufficient; composition should span general + math + code, plus your tool-call/agentic domain to match workload. Token count and on-policy freshness matter more than example count. — [https://arxiv.org/pdf/2509.18362](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2509.18362) _(conf high)_
- Quantizing the TARGET has minimal impact on draft accept rate for weight-only W4A16/W8A8 (near-lossless, no retrain needed) — BUT this evidence does NOT extend to NVFP4's 4-bit activations; aggressive activation quantization (W4A8) showed the largest degradation, so a fine-tune IS warranted for your FP4 case. — [https://arxiv.org/abs/2505.22179](https://arxiv.org/abs/2505.22179) _(conf high)_
- The DeepSeek MTP head is inherently lean (shares the base embedding + output head, adds only one Transformer block TRM_k + a d×2d projection M_k per depth; ~3% of params), which is ideal for a standalone pure-CUDA offline trainer that freezes the backbone and updates only the head. — [https://arxiv.org/pdf/2412.19437](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2412.19437) _(conf high)_
- Multi-step training-consistency techniques (HASS, CORAL, EAGLE-style training-time test) close the train/inference gap and set the achievable speedup ceiling (2.5x-4.07x) — worth adding once the basic warm-start fine-tune works, but not required for the first lean pass. — [https://arxiv.org/abs/2408.15766](https://arxiv.org/abs/2408.15766) _(conf high)_
- Achievable target for a ~150-200B MoE after a short fine-tune: acceptance length τ in the ~4-5.7 range and end-to-end decode speedup ~2.2-2.95x are realistic, based on the closest analog (DeepSeek-V3 685B MoE MTP head, 1-epoch KL fine-tune). — [https://arxiv.org/pdf/2602.23881](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2602.23881) _(conf medium)_
- Self-speculative / pruning-aware drafting (build the draft by pruning the target itself) is a zero-training fallback baseline, but yields lower speedup than a trained head. — [https://arxiv.org/pdf/2602.05499](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2602.05499) _(conf medium)_
- A target-independent draft (train once, reuse across a model family) is possible via stochastic gating of target-feature injection, but costs ~15-20% of accepted-token performance vs a target-dependent head. — [https://arxiv.org/pdf/2605.08632](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2605.08632) _(conf medium)_
